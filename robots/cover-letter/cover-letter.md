We are pleased to submit our manuscript, “Deploy, Augment, or Hire? A Total-Cost-of-Ownership and Cohort Framework for Robot-Labor Deployment Decisions Across Global Wage Regions,” for consideration as a Research Article in Science Robotics. The work reframes the robot-versus-labor decision from one of capital acquisition to one of deployment economics, and provides a transparent, fully parameterized framework that any reader can re-run with their own inputs.

Significance. Most published comparisons set a robot’s sticker price against a bare hourly wage. We instead model full robot total cost of ownership (manufactured cost, deployment, a service multiple, operating cost, and a refurbish-or-retire rule) against properly loaded labor cost, and we keep rigorously separate the two senses of “depreciation” on each side of the comparison: physical wear of capital equipment versus Becker-Mincer decay of human skills. Instantiated across seven wage regions and three skill cohorts, the framework shows that automation favorability is strongly regional and cohort-specific, that utilization and shift coverage—not hardware price—are the dominant levers, and that the dominant rational outcome is augmentation rather than wholesale displacement. We believe these results will interest the journal’s broad readership across robotics, automation economics, and labor policy.

Originality and ethical framing. This manuscript is original, has not been published elsewhere, and is not under consideration by any other journal. All numerical anchors flagged as illustrative or user-provided are identified as such in the text, and the analysis is bounded by an explicit ethical guardrail: lifecycle, depreciation, and retirement reasoning apply only to capital equipment and never to people.
